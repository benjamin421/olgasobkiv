Theatrical Illusions

The proposed theater is sited within the masonry-rich context of St. Louis. Theatrical Illusions proposes a play on traditional brick exteriors by using extrusion to incorporate light and luminosity. The final theater features a central lobby, box office, two double helix ramps to a central amphitheater, and a upper-level promenade. The double-helix promenade at the exterior of the main theater core, positions program users between the solidity of the concrete theater walls and the airy extruded masonry façade. The design was inspired by the curved and rectilinear forms superimposed by Le Corbusier in floor plans for the Villa Schwob. A series of trace drawings and Bristol models were made to analyze the overall composition interactions within the Villa Schwob - symmetry, asymmetry, shadow, and gestalt forms. A volumetric grid model was built to understand the interaction of three-dimensional planes and introduced extrusion as a final design element.
